Case Study 2: Modernizing a Family Business

The Challenge: Managing a Business Without Clear Data

Role: Project Lead & Developer (Low-code)

Stack: Google AppSheet, Google Sheets

My family’s garden and landscaping supply shop has been a stable business for years. However, it relied entirely on manual paper records and the manager's intuition. When I joined, I noticed a few areas that could be improved.

  • Intuition over Data: Procurement was based on "eyeballing" stock levels rather than actual sales data, leading to frequent out-of-stock scenarios and missed sales opportunities.
  • The Owner’s Dependency: To know what was happening in the shop, an owner or manager had to be physically present. There was no way to check sales or stock levels from outside.
  • Unusable Data: Unusable Data: While daily sales were recorded on paper, making it very hard to analyze which products drove the best cash flow or profit margins.
The Strategy: A Simple Digital Tool, Low-Code Approach

I decided to build a custom POS (Point of Sale) solution using AppSheet. This allowed me to prioritize two things: Cost-efficiency and Ease of Use (UX) for a non-technical workforce.

I. Step-by-Step Improvements

I didn't change everything at once. I divided the work into two phases to make it easier for the team:

  • Phase 1 (Core Operations): Digital Product Catalog, PO/SO Management, and Daily/Monthly Summaries.
  • Phase 2 (Optimization): Low-stock alerts, Staff Attendance & Payroll calculation, and Digital Invoicing.

II. Helping the Team Adapt

The biggest goal was to make sure the staff felt comfortable using the new tool. I used a few simple strategies:

  • Familiar Form Factor: Since the staff already used smartphones, a tablet felt more natural to them than a computer.
  • Clean and Simple Design: I designed a "Clean UI" I only showed the buttons and information they absolutely needed based on their roles (Admin vs. Sales). This made the app easy to learn and reduced mistakes.
  • Focus on Benefits: I framed the system not as a "monitoring tool," but as a "helper" that saved them from doing long manual calculations at the end of each day.

The Result: Real-time Remote Oversight & Data-Driven Growth

The transformation has moved the business from a traditional shop to a modern, scalable, and data-driven operation that I can now monitor from anywhere.

  • Remote Tracking: I can now see real-time sales and stock levels from my phone, even when I'm not at the shop.
  • Accurate Inventory: By tracking sales digitally, we reduced stock mistakes to less than 2%, despite dealing with non-barcoded items (like clay pots and soil).
  • Role-Based Access Control: Implemented security layers to ensure data integrity and prevent unauthorized access to sensitive financial records.
  • Better Decisions: We now have a clear list of what sells best. We no longer guess what to buy, but use the data to make smarter choices for the business.

Personal Takeaway

This project was a masterclass in Pragmatic Problem Solving. It taught me that Digital Transformation doesn't have to be complicated or expensive. It’s about understanding the real problems of a business and creating a tool that people enjoy using and managing the transition with empathy and strategy. By building this system, I’ve made the business more stable and gained back my time to pursue new opportunities.

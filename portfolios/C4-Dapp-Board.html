Experimental: DappBoard

Simplifying the Multi-Chain Experience for Everyone

Overview

In 2021, the Crypto landscape shifted from being Ethereum-centric to a fragmented "Multi-Chain" world (L1s and L2s). While innovation flourished, the user experience suffered. Investors had assets scattered across various protocols and chains, forcing them to manually track transactions in Excel or keep many browser tabs open. DappBoard was designed to solve this fragmentation pain by consolidating complex on-chain data into a single, intuitive interface.

The Problem: The "Messy" Era of DeFi

Before centralized trackers became mainstream, being an active on-chain user was a manual chore:

  • Data Fragmentation: Users had to visit separate block explorers (Etherscan, BscScan, etc.) to see their balances.
  • Excel Fatigue: Manual logging was prone to error and lacked real-time accuracy.
  • The Barrier to Entry: For newcomers, the technical complexity of bridging and tracking assets was a massive wall preventing adoption.

The Solution: Design for Experts, Simplify for Beginners

My goal was to build a tool that Hardcore On-Chain users could use to save time, while making it simple enough that Beginners wouldn't need to understand the underlying blockchain tech to see their net worth.

I. Strategic Data Hierarchy

I prioritized information based on cognitive load. On a limited mobile screen or a wide desktop, the order remains consistent:

  • Total Balance: The most critical metric, displayed at the top.
  • Portfolio Trend: A visual chart (1D to All-time) to show growth or volatility.
  • Asset Allocation by Chain: A visual breakdown of where the money lives. I designed a Global Filter by clicking or hovering on a specific chain, the entire dashboard (Balance, Charts, Protocol details) updates to show data for only that chain.
II. Managing Complexity through Filtering & Grouping

To prevent "Information Overload" the UI utilizes two main organizational strategies:

  • Protocol Grouping: Assets aren't just listed as tokens, they are grouped by the Protocol they are interacting with (e.g., Staking, Yield Farming). This shows "Active" vs. "Idle" assets.
  • Multi-Chain Unified View: Users can see everything combined or use the filter system to drill down into specific ecosystems like Arbitrum or BSC.

Key Features

  • Dynamic Dashboard: Real-time asset tracking across multiple chains.
  • Yield & Reward Tracking: Detailed view of performance and accumulated rewards from DeFi protocols.
  • Advanced Statistics: Portfolio exposure analysis and "Best/Worst Performer" tracking.
  • Unified Transaction History: A single chronological feed of activities across all connected chains.

Visual Identity & Implementation

  • Themes: Designed in both Dark and Light Modes. Knowing CSS, I built the design system using Color Variables, ensuring that the transition between themes is seamless and developer-friendly.
  • Tooling: Prototyped in Figma, utilizing advanced plugins for data visualization (charts and tables) to ensure the mockups reflected realistic data densities.

Key Takeaways: Making Hard Data Look Easy

Designing DappBoard taught me three vital lessons in handling complex data:

  • Visual Shortcuts: Humans process colors, icons, and shapes faster than text and numbers. Using charts and chain-specific icons allows users to scan their portfolio in seconds.
  • Structured Clarity: Tables are the best way to present deep details, provided they are organized with a clear pattern and logical spacing.
  • User Control: A robust Filter System is the best way to manage data noise. By giving users the power to hide what they don't need, the interface remains clean regardless of how many assets they own.
